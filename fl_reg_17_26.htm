Active Registered Voters in Florida

On July 29,2025, an article was posted on the Florida Politics website titled "Miami-Dade Dems accuse Elections Supervisor of pro-GOP bias, trying to 'steal' local elections". The article begins:

Leaders of the Miami-Dade Democratic Party are accusing recently elected Supervisor of Elections Alina Garcia, a longtime Republican operative and former state lawmaker, of using her new post to tilt the county's voter composition in her party's favor.

They're now diving into public records, they say, to determine whether - and to what extent - the Supervisor of Elections (SOE) Office has manipulated voter rolls since November.

Further on, the article states the following numbers:

A side-by-side comparison of active voter numbers from February and those from July, after an off-year voter roll maintenance that occurred statewide, shows Miami-Dade shed a 106,435 Democratic and 105,183 NPA voters - 41% each of the net total 259,606-voter reduction - while removing just 47,988 Republicans (18% of the total) over the same period.

These numbers are contained in the following table taken from the article:

App for Viewing Florida Registration

The numbers given can be verified via an online app at https://econdata.shinyapps.io/reg11/. Going to that URL will bring up the following page:

The above plot shows the change in the number of active registered voters in Florida since 2017. As can be seen, the change in the number of Republican and Democratic voters since 2017 remained similar until mid-2020, just before the 2020 General Election. Since then, the number of Republican active registered voters have increased modestly while the number of Democratic active registered voters has declined sharply.

Unchecking the 'Calculate change' checkbox will now result in the following plot:

The above plot is showing the actual number of active registered voters. As can be seen, there were slightly more Democratic than Republican voters until 2021. However, in October of 2021, the number of Republican voters surpassed the number of Democratic voters with the former continuing to increase while the latter declined.

Viewing a Single Party in Multiple Counties

Rather than viewing all parties for a single county, it's possible to view multiple counties for a single party by setting the 'Plot counties' checkbox. Starting again at the initial page, ensuring that the PARTY select list is set to DEM and setting the 'Plot counties' checkbox, and hovering the cursor over the rightmost point in the black line will result in the following plot:

This plot shows the number of DEM ARVs (active registered voters) for the 10 Florida counties with the most total ARVs. As can be seen, the number of active registered DEM voters in Miami-Dade has dropped about 186,427 since 2017. Hovering over the points at the bottom of the drops in voters show that large drops occurred in Miami-Dade County in May of 2025, in Hillsborough, Pinellas, and Polk Counties a month earlier in April of 2025, in Broward County in December of 2023 and December of 2025, and in a number of other times and places.

Unchecking the 'Calculate change' checkbox will now result in the following plot:

This plot shows that DEM ARVs rose together from about 600 thousand in 2017 to about 635 thousand in November of 2020 and then began a gradual decline. That decline was not in sync as the two counties seem to have voter roll purges at differing times. Most of the other counties followed the same pattern of peaking around the time of the 2020 General election and steadily declining since then.

Viewing Multiple Counties via the Arrow Keys

Another way to quickly view and compare multiple counties is with the up and down arrow keys under the COUNTY select list. Starting at the initial page, setting COUNTY to LAFAYETTE, and unchecking the 'Calculate change' checkbox will result in the following plot:

This plot shows that the party with the most ARVs (active registered voters) in Lafayette County switch from Democratic to Republican in September of 2019. Like Miami, the numbers for both parties dropped in apparent voter roll purges in December of 2023 and June of 2025. It is odd, however, is how the two parties seemed to mirror each other up until the first purge in December of 2023. Especially odd is June 2020 when the number of DEM ARVs declined by 333 as the number of REP ARVs increased by 381. The drop of 333 DEM ARVs would seem to indicate a purge but this is contradicted by the rise of 381 REP ARVs. The most likely benign explanation that I can think of is that 300-plus voters suddenly decided to switch parties that month. This could likely be verified by examining the voter rolls for that month.

Viewing Counties with the 'Plot percent' Option

By default, the y-axis will show the change in ARVs (active registered voters) or the number of ARVs as a count. If the 'Plot percent' checkbox is selected, however, the y-axis will show these values as a percent. Starting at the initial page amd setting COUNTY to BROWARD will result in the following plot:

In the above plot, it can be seen that the DEM ARVs decrease more than the REP ARVs in each of the purges in May of 2022, December of 2023, and December of 2025 in Broward County. However, there are many more DEM ARVs in that county so the comparison may be misleading. Selecting the 'Plot percent' checkbox will change the plot as follows:

This plot now shows the change in the ARVs as a percent of the level in 2017. As a percent, it appears that the DEM and REP ARVs drop by about the same amount during the purges except for the second purge (December of 2023) when the DEM ARVs look to drop a bit more. However, the No-party ARVs appear to have dropped much more of a percentage in that purge and the next one in December of 2025.

Unchecking the 'Calculate change' checbox now will change the plot as follows:

First, it should be noted that the value of the points now equal the percent of the total (100 percent) that each ARV equals. For this reason and the fact that all of the parties dropped by about the same percentage during the first purge in May of 2022, that first purge is not visible in the plot. That is, since each party fell by the same percentage, they all continued to comprise the same percentage of the total as before. However, the second and third purges are visible as the No-party ARV going down by about the same percentage as the Republican party went up. This would occur if a significant number of ARVs switched from No-party to Republican in the same month of the purge. This would seem highly coicidental so it seems likely that the explanation is something else.
